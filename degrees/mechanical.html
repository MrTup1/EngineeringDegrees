What is Mechanical Engineering?
If you’re interested in moving parts and designing physical components, Mechanical Engineering is worth diving into. Mechanical engineering is the study of objects and systems in motion. It’s the study of machinery, and how to manufacture and maintain it at all levels. Mechanical engineering is a branch of engineering that deals with the design, manufacturing, and maintenance of mechanical systems, devices, and machinery. Mechanical engineers use their knowledge of mechanics, thermodynamics, and materials science to develop and improve a wide range of products and processes..
What do Mechanical Engineers do?
Mechanical Engineering is a very broad profession, every engineer will have a different experience working. Most engineers are either responsible for designing, analysing and overseeing projects involving moving components. You can either specialise in a particular field (e.g. Thermodynamics…) or be a generalist. They use CAD (Computer Aided Design) regularly in their workflow and use an array of different software to test their design’s effectiveness by running simulations.

Mechanical Engineer
Most mechanical degree holders become mechanical engineers. Mechanical engineers create and develop physical products involving mechanics. This includes generators, engines, control systems, sensors and so much more.
Mechanical Technician
Mechanical technicians assist with the iteration, testing and making of mechanical machinery. Their main responsibility is to have a deep understanding of the machinery and troubleshoot any machinery that malfunctions. In addition, they are also responsible for creating maintenance plans that prevent malfunctions and guarantee the safety of any users around them.
Design Engineer
Design engineers use computer aided software to create and design new innovative products through research and development. They always have a set of design criteria such as cost, user experience … which they will use as a guide to create prototypes that improve upon existing products/processes.
Maintenance Engineer
Maintenance engineers typically are responsible for the continuous running of all machinery and tools within industries. They regularly create maintenance routines and methods to achieve their main goal, through carefully thought-out strategies. They also ensure that all processes comply with the countries’ health and safety regulations.
Marine Engineer
Marine engineers produce mechanical equipment for any watercraft such as boats and submarines. They basically do the same design, analysis and testing stage as other engineers do, only in a marine setting with additional protocols.
