What is Aerospace Engineering?
If you’re interested in any flying aircraft such as planes or designing rockets, Aerospace or Aeronautical Engineering should be your priority. Aerospace engineering is the study of engineering dealing with the design, iterative development and manufacture of any aircraft or spacecraft. Their main diverging branches are Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering.
What is the difference between Aeronautical and Astronautical Engineering?

Aeronautical Engineer
Aeronautical engineers design, create and maintain aircraft such as helicopters and missiles. They frequently carry out investigations of the aircraft to check for any structural faults by conducting simulations with flight and weather condition, to guarantee that the aircraft is safe for human use. In addition, they also often maintain and repair aircraft after recent damage.
Astronautical Engineer
Astronautical engineers design, develop and operate spacecraft beyond the earth’s atmosphere. They often innovate upon existing technologies and are at the cutting edge of technology. These could include sophisticated propulsion systems or new ways for guiding spacecraft during a mission.
Aerospace Technician
Aerospace technicians are mainly responsible for assembling aircrafts or spacecrafts, such as wings, engines using a wide array of machinery. They are also critical for the regular inspection and testing of aircrafts and spacecrafts to ensure they meet safety standards.
