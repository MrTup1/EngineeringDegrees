What is Environmental Engineering?
If you want to make actual positive changes towards the environment, environmental engineering is for you. Environmental Engineers use principles of engineering to develop solutions to environmental problems such as global warming haunting us today.
Environmental Engineering VS Civil Engineering
Environmental Engineering is extremely similar compared to Civil Engineering, but there are differences. There major difference is in how environmental engineers will learn about chemistry and biology while civil engineers focus on different infrastructures such as trains. Environmental Engineers also typically learn about advanced water supply systems. Many universities only offer this course as a master as there is so much overlap, or combine it with civil engineering.

Water Project Manager
Water project managers oversee projects correlated to water or wastewater management. They deal with ever intensifying problems such as drought and water pollution. They perform many duties, such as analyzing water supplies, designing distributions systems… They also often oversee if other technicians are conducting their work correctly and they are cautious of any environmental impacts the project will have.
Environmental health and safety director
They are mainly hired to ensure firms comply with safety regulations and industry standards, and they minimize hazards from processes. They are tasked to identify any hazardous biological, chemical, and radiological materials to analyze and identify potential risks.
Green building Engineer
Green building engineers design ways to reduce emissions and increase the overall efficiency of a building’s operations, without sacrificing economic efficiency.
